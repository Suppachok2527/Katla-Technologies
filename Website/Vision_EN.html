Vision

Make water clean again!

  

In addition to large plastic parts in the sea, microplastic particles pose an enormous risk to humans and nature.

In contrast to large plastic parts, it has so far been difficult to get the microparticles out of the water.
The large filter nets to tackle the problem, contrary to their purpose, are destroying habitats and killing sea creatures.

Our idea is to copy the already perfect body of the largest sea dweller (the blue whale) and use its impressive water flow for our purposes.
Instead of krill like the blue whale, we filter microplastic particles out of the water and do not endanger neither habitats nor marine life.

Efficient design and untouchable


Due to its size alone, the blue whale is hardly hunted by other sea creatures.
With a few technical upgrades (short-term interference signal), the last few rare attacks can be stopped.
We are still at the beginning of the development.

Our goal in the field of robotics

The ultimate goal is a robotic whale that filters microplastics and small plastics out of the water instead of plankton.
In contrast to conventional nets with the same goals, no other life forms would die with our method.

All marine animals that can counteract the migration of a blue whale seem to do so instinctively.

Nets also drag on the seabed and therefore destroy habitats. On this page, you can see our progress in robotics.
Difficulties here:

  • Vision
  • Autonomous safe behavior under water
  • Filter system
